Sub-mm Data Reduction!

Advisors: Dr. Thomas Nikola and Prof. Gordon Stacey

For my project, I designed a Graphical User Interface that the Sub-mm Astrophysics group uses to produce, display, and analyze spectra from their ZEUS-2 spectrometer.

I also utiilized Python's NumPy package to refine the efficiency of the data processing and implemented different methods for co-adding the spectral data for each pixel.

            

A screenshot of the top half of the GUI.                
On the left, there are options for selecting data, clears, and filtering options, among other things.                
On the right, the GUI displays spectra across ZEUS-2's 2-D bolometer array of pixels.                
Users can select different pixels to view the data time series for that pixel,               
and also different rows to view the 1-D spectra for a particular row.                

            

An example time series for a particular pixel. ZEUS-2 is observing the source when the red line is 1.                

With the GUI, the Professor Stacey's research group can more easily harness the data fromt their ZEUS-2 instrument to study sub-mm astrophysics.

You can read more about what the group studies in the sub-mm wavelengths here.
