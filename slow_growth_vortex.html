Slowly-growing Planet-induced Vortices!

Advisors: Prof. Kaitlin Kratter and Dr. Min-Kai Lin

For my project, I explored whether gas giant planets that form on realistic timescales can still induce vortices.

Gap-opening planets can trigger vortices through the Rossby Wave Instability at their gap edges. This scenario has been proposed as an explanation for asymmetric features in protoplanetary disks observed with ALMA, but it has previously only been simulated with planets growing on unrealistically short timescales.

             

Rapidly-grown planet-induced vortex.                     Slowly-grown planet-induced vortex.             

I used FARGO to simulate a giant planet opening up a gap with different growth times. I found that the even with relatively short growth times of ~1000 orbits, planets can only induce vortices with much lower densities, wider azimuths, and shorter lifetimes.

                  

Vortex lifetimes (left) and densities (right) for different planet masses, disk viscosities, and growth times.
