LEAPS!

Advisors: Dr. Lucie Jilkova and Prof. Simon Portegies Zwart

For my project, I investigated which types of stellar encounters could result in a passing-by star transferring objects from its circumstellar disk into our Solar System.

This could have only happened in the Sun's birth cluster. An encounter close enough to transfer objects would still be unlikely. If it did happen, it could leave a signature.

             

A stellar encounter! The Sun is in motion.          Here in a different view, the Sun is at the center.      

I used an N-Body code in AMUSE to simulate stellar encounters with different flyby star masses, distances of closest approach, (and flyby eccentricities and inclinations).

I found that the lowest mass stars can transfer objects at distances of up to 400 AU.

            

This plot shows the percentage of the flyby star's disk that transfers to the Sun's disk                
for different masses of the flyby star and different distances of closest approach for the flyby orbit.
