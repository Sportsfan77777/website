REUs

Do I need to participate in an REU?

No. You can also do summer research at your university!***

(1) If you do summer research at your university, you can easily work on your project for more than just ~10 weeks in the summer (You can start early and/or continue after the summer ends). Working on a single project for a longer time often gives you the chance to accomplish more. With an REU, you may have an opportunity to continue your work after the summer ends, but this is almost always done remotely.

(2) At your university, you can choose who you want to work with. Most REUs simply assign you an advisor (often, based on what you tell them, but you usually do not have the final say). Depending on your research interests, this may or may not be important to you.

(3) You may not be able to get an offer for an REU summer position. This is okay and nothing against you. There are not that many REU positions and who gets offered positions often depends on "fit" that is out of your control.

(4) Note that most freshmen do not get REU offers. Though, if you stay at your university and do research the summmer following your freshman year, you will be in an excellent position and much more likely to get an REU as a sophomore (or later on, as a junior.)

***If you are at a university without a stand-alone astronomy department or not too many research opportunities for undergraduates, try also looking for additional opportunities elsewhere beyond conventional summer research programs. If you have a source of funding (which your university or other fellowships may be able to provide), individual researchers may be open to taking summer students.***
