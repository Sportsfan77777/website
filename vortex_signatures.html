Signatures of Planet-induced Vortices!

Advisors: Dr. Paola Pinilla and Prof. Kaitlin Kratter

In this project, I expanded on my previous work by simulating the dust evolution in elongated planet-induced vortices in order to identify their signatures in observations.

Although these vortices still trap dust, I found that they do not trap dust directly in their centers because their gas structure lacks a smooth pressure bump and is disrupted by the planet's spiral density waves. As a result, elongated planet-induced vortices are identifiable by their (1) wide azimuthal extents and (2) off-center peaks.
