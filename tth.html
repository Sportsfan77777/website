The Triple Helix! (TTH!)

I wrote one article for Cornell's chapter of The Triple Helix, a Science in Society journal, on the state of human spaceflight in the United States shortly after the shutdown of the Space Shuttle program.

A scan of that article is linked below.

            

I wrote The World Without the Space Shutle.                

My Comments (Several Years Later):

A lot has changed regarding human spaceflight since I wrote that article.
SpaceX has "taken off" substantially, and the Dragon looks poised to be the first spacecraft to succeed the Space Shuttle within a few years. Their reusable rockets also look very promising.
Meanwhile, the US government has re-devoted resources to continue the development of the Orion and Space Launch System vehicles.

Where will human spaceflight be 10 years from now?
It depends on our commitment to these projects.
